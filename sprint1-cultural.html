I was living on my own, when a friend of mine from around the village asked if she could stay at mine as she needed to move out of where she was at. I had been warned by another friend that she had previously lived with that she is a terrible flatmate and will use me and not resect my homes rules.

• Describe how your culture has influenced your values and identity.

I was born and raised in Zimbabwe, where I attended boarding school from the age of six. I was therefore raised by various people. From my mum I was taught resilience, absolute love, and how to haggle like a pro at the flea markets. My Dad taught me about hard work, and the constellations in the night sky, amongst various things like how to check and record the humidity in a tobacco barn(when we hung out it was usually when he was working) . Our gardener taught me how to grow plants, and to appreciate where our food comes from, and how to make bows and arrows, dig holes, and make petrol can guitars. Our maid taught me how to laugh in life, about the Shona culture (I often rode my bike down to visit her daughter at the compound, where I would sometimes have lunch in their hut), and appreciation for what we have. From my Matrons and superintendents at boarding school and just from being at boarding school, I was taught about punctuality, organization, cleanliness, social skills, manners, and respect. Africa taught me a lot of my core values, it is a place where women shoot guns with the men, fought alongside the men, hunted with the men, rode motorbikes like the men, swore like the men and drank and smoked like the men (and sailors at that). However somehow in a marriage a women’s place was still the kitchen and to be subservient to their husbands. It was a magical place, with a very unique set of life lessons. I feel like what I am trying to do now is not to only better myself to something unknown to me, but to re find a previous version of myself and re-establish the true me that was lost along the way, and build upon that with new life lessons which I have gained from various experiences throughout difficult years (Minus the addictions and self-destructive behaviours).

• Share an example from your experience of where you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss the strategies you used at that time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now, and why.

On the last course that I did at Weltec, a group of us were trying to do a project to present to the rest of the class in the form of a brain storm of sorts. However one of the members in the group kept taking over and over riding everyone else’s suggestions despite the fact that he was not only incorrect in the information he was adding but others had gathered a lot more valid, and useful material that he was disregarding. The strategy used was basically to let him do it (I’m sure there was a lot of eye rolling) and we just learned of each other. We presented “his” brainstorm, and the teacher knowing what he was like could tell what had gone on, and made some stabbing remark toward him, about being controlling etc. This was not dealt with properly by any of us including the teacher. If I were to go through that again the strategy that I would most likely try instead would be to, calmly (but firmly) bring to attention the fact that we all have contributions to make and suggest that we make an equal amount of comments on the presentation.
